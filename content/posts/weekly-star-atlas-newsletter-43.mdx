The last week was exciting, with many new images and short clips being shared with the community, illustrating the progress made on SCREAM and the Showroom. Furthermore, there was a [new Townhall (#33)](https://youtu.be/djT5niHIGAs) with two guests from the team: Brad (UE5 Gameplay Engineer) & Chris (Director of Game Economy - focus on SCREAM). Lastly, a [new issue of the Atlas Star](https://medium.com/star-atlas/the-atlas-star-issue-10-59630af15c91) was published, presenting us with a deep dive into the lore that surrounds the Cataclysm at the center of Star Atlas!

## 426Live

There was also some bad news. Before the Townhall started, there had been some hints that we might hear more about the next 426Live event. During the event, Michael (CEO) admitted that they had indeed planned to announce the date, but that unfortunately a complication had surfaced on that very Friday that made the team backtrack on that.

The team did not go into any detail of what this complication entailed exactly.

## Volant Studio Showroom

While we wait for the distribution of the initial release of the Showroom, the team is working hard on the second release (R2). This release will come with multi-player support and limited flight capabilities (Pearce X4 only). The team released a video that shows the [mounting animation for a Pearce X4](https://twitter.com/aephia/status/1564232156586676225?s=20&t=lHr-X1OWcRfVq2DCL6gwvw).
## Prototype

In addition to that, the team shared another video showing off one of their developers (Brad) [flying a Pearce X4 through an obstacle course](https://twitter.com/aephia/status/1564258209422053377?s=20&t=hla0YpvDRsde5ngxyuyF8A), with stationary turrets shooting electrical charges at them. A hit of these would make him lose speed.

Note that we may not see this exact gameplay end up in future game modules, but it's sure nice to see some more of these prototypes the team is working on.

## Progress

In their weekly roadmap report, the team shared that the blockchain programs for Cargo, Crafting, Player Profile, *[Loyalty]* Points & *[Loyalty]* Points Store are almost ready. Ship escrowing has been completed and they have been busy finalizing movement. Additionally, the team started implementing programs to allow crafting at a starbase, and fleet configuration.

The economy design team has been researching starbase crafting and upgrading. And finally, the web team has started working on the "Outliner UI" and updating warp and impulse movement functionality.
## Features

Some other tidbits we got last week from Chip (Game Systems Designer):
- If you emergency-escape (flee) then you will end up in an adjacent sector.
- You don't earn LP for attacking someone in open space because that would be easily abused via kill trading. Open space is when you arenâ€™t assigned to a planet or starbase.
- Starbase battles and planets will likely be large-scale timed events. Open space single target battles are small-scale timed events. Smaller timer to enter and help your buddy out if they get attacked.
- Battles automatically resolve with the fleets that have elected to join once the timer hits zero.
- You can defend starbases by clicking on the starbase and selecting "Defend".
