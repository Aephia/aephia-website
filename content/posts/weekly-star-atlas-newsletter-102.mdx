## SAGE Labs

There have been multiple exciting bits of news regarding SAGE Labs. Not only did the team release a patch that came with big changes, but Michael also shared a good bit about the present and future of SAGE on the Atlas Brew last week.

### Patch

Last Thursday, October 19th, a new patch for SAGE Labs went live. The most significant impact is related to scanning. Not only have toolkit costs been updated (often lowered) for most ships, but the Data Runner ships can now scan for free!

Of course, only one Data Runner ship is currently available, the VZUS opod (medium size). But the team is looking to change that this week. See the next section for more information on that.

Here are the patch notes:

* Data Runners no longer consume toolkits when scanning.
* Refilled Fimbul Airbike and CSS Tier 1 hab recipes with more resource-intensive redemption recipe options
* Updated scanning cooldowns to be based on heatsink manufacturer, spec bonus, and tier
* Updated toolkit consumption when scanning to be based on scanner manufacturer, spec bonus, and tier
* Reduced R9 mining rates

### Atlas Brew

Last Wednesday, Michael (CEO) was a guest on [the Atlas Brew (#84)](https://youtu.be/VMdtWS9f3K0?si=RrpHzxKNC-Zokkf8), answering questions about SAGE Labs and the future.

He started by sharing that they probably put more resources into Labs than they should have. But the team is learning a lot from Labs being live, and they will continue to fix things that will also improve the future iterations of SAGE. New features are currently on hold for SAGE Labs, however.

Meanwhile, most of the development team has transitioned to SAGE Starbased, the 3D browser game the team wants to move forward with.

On that note, Michael backtracked a bit regarding keeping SAGE Labs up-to-date next to Starbased. As the team sees the 3D SAGE game as their browser-based end-product, they may not want to spend manpower on keeping SAGE Labs up to date alongside it.

This means the next anticipated feature, Starbase construction & upgrading, will launch together with Starbased. It is not currently being developed for Labs, so it is unclear whether Labs will be updated to include it. It could be that Starbased will even launch before Starbase construction. In that case, it will have full feature parity with Labs and will not introduce any new features upon launch. 

One front-end developer, Xeanor, has been working on Front-end optimizations for mobile, while a few others are wrapping up the first MVP for DAC support.

## Foundation Room Changes

Those who have been around on the Star Atlas Discord server have undoubtedly heard of the Foundation Room channel. This channel has been available to everyone who (Nitro) boosted the Discord server for the longest time.

Of course, boosting a server requires a Discord Nitro subscription, meaning your money will go to Discord, not the team.

This was changed last week. Everyone who had access before this change will find they have received an ‚ÄúFR ‚Äì OG‚Äù role, guaranteeing they will retain access. However, it will not be possible to boost the server and gain access that way.

The team worked with Matrica to bring three new ways to get access:

* You own a wallet with 80k Polis Voting Power (PVP)
* You‚Äôve been quite active on the Star Atlas Discord and have achieved level 45 (Mee6)
* You completed Never Alone quests and collected memory fragments.
