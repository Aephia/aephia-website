## Never Alone Rewards

It took the team a little over 2 years, but the Never Alone rewards will be claimable very soon through metacrafting recipes accessible in SAGE Starbase.

As a refresher, the Never Alone campaign was hosted on [Zealy](https://zealy.io/cw/staratlas-never-alone) and consisted of answering questions (some within a specified time window) and performing social media actions. New questions were added throughout the campaign, so participants had to stay on top of things.

By doing so, people would earn XP on Zealy, and when they unlocked a certain amount, they could claim an on-chain memory. This would consist of Glimpses (first level), Fragments (second level), and Revelations (last level) for each of the 3 seasons (dubbed ‚ÄúMissions‚Äù) the team hosted.

The particularly cool thing here was that the individual quests all shared lore bits. Throughout the quests, a story evolved, in which a punaab named _Saand_, who lost his memory, slowly regained bits, while looking for clues to his past.

One of the fun aspects of this campaign is therefore that the Saand character is one of the prizes that can be claimed.

Over the three missions, 9 individual memory shards could be earned, 3 different glimpses, 3 fragments, and 3 revelations. These can be redeemed individually for a specific reward, or grouped in sets of 3 (all mission glimpses, fragments, or revelations) for a bag of rewards.

We‚Äôre listing all rewards once again below, starting with those that can be redeemed for individual memory shards:

**Mission 1:**

* Memory Glimpse: Exclusive Character Skin,
* Memory Fragment: VZUS Solos,
* Memory Revelation: Exclusive VZUS Solos Ship Skin

**Mission 2:**

* Memory Glimpse: Exclusive Never Alone Emote
* Memory Fragment: Fimbul ECOS Unibomba
* Memory Revelation: Exclusive CORE Century Cover

As with the previous Council elections, the voting mechanism used here is the Single Transferable Vote (STV). This means you will be able to select up to 5 candidates in order, and if the PVP assigned to your top choice exceeds the quota, the surplus is divided pro rata among the voters‚Äô second choices. This process repeats until five candidates have been elected.

As with the previous election PIPs, you can see the STV (Single Transferable Vote) algorithm in action using our [visualizer tool](https://stv.aephia.com/).
