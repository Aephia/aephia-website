## Z.ink O.RIGINS Airdrop

Michael (CEO) shared last week that the current thinking is to drop roughly **5% up front** and have the remaining **95%** of each player’s airdrop **vested over six months or more**. There will be an early-claim penalty that sends the forfeited balance **back into the pool** for people willing to vest longer. This should keep the circulating supply relatively tight at the start, while tokenholders will have a great incentive to stake their z.ink tokens given that the chain’s inflation curve is expected to be highest at the start.

## Holosim

John (Product Manager) spent another week sanding down the parts of **Holosim Chapter 2** that had been causing the most friction. The biggest change is simple: destroyed fleets now have more than one bad option. The new **Minimum Repair** button lets players spend a single toolkit to get moving again without fully restoring the fleet, while **Rescue Crew** gives them a way to pull crew out safely instead of being stuck behind a full repair bill. That follows the repair-cost overhaul we [covered in #224](https://aephia.com/star-atlas/weekly-star-atlas-newsletter-224/), but this week’s changes feel more practical and a lot less punitive.

A few other clarifications mattered too. John said **fleet-to-fleet repairs** are now working mechanically, with dedicated repair ships restoring more HP per toolkit through their `repairAbility` stat. He also confirmed that the current **AP cooldowns** are only enforced in the UI for now and can still be bypassed on-chain, because he wants more testing before locking that behavior into the blockchain program itself. The **warp cooldown**, meanwhile, is there to slow excess resource production and inflation, not just to make movement take longer.

Live testing of the last storyline quest has also been moving ahead. The team carved out the middle of the map as a temporary no-fly zone while running tests for the **Superphoenix** event, and that restriction was later extended by another 72 hours. If this testing is successful, we should see the ECOS Superphoenix pop up on the map soon!

### Holosim Changelog

This week saw only one official change going live. We included the changelog below:

**v1.2.25**

* Fleet repairs no longer eat your whole toolkit stack — only the toolkits actually needed are used
* Repair ATLAS costs now line up correctly with how much HP you repair
* Added Rescue Crew for destroyed fleets — do a quick minimum repair, then pull crew out safely
* Added Minimum Repair and Cancel Installation buttons in upgrade/crafting flows so you have a recovery option if something gets stuck
* Fixed upgrade submit failures caused by out-of-sync points epochs — you now get a retry prompt instead of a hard on-chain error

## UE5

The team shared two short but interesting updates on the ongoing UE5 work. The **Galia single-player** game mode is set to receive an interesting addition soon, so-called **Elimination** missions. This is the first use case of the team’s new AI Navigation on Planet Surfaces. To demonstrate this, they [shared a new video](https://x.com/staratlas/status/2042377135848112129?s=20) \[see below\] on X that shows an outpost (overrun by Jorvik) on the dark side of _Merevia_. Your task: eliminate their leader.
