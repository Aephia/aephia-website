Today marks a milestone that’s hard to put into words.

Five years ago, on **July 18th**, **2021**, Aephia Industries was founded.

Back then, Star Atlas was little more than an ambitious vision. There was no playable universe, no DAO, no browser game, and no way of knowing whether any of it would ever become reality. There were only white papers, concept art, and a community of people who believed that one day it could become something extraordinary.

We were fortunate enough to be among them.

From day one, our ambition was never simply to become another guild.

**We wanted to build an organization.**

An organization that could one day thrive inside the Star Atlas universe. One that would contribute to the ecosystem, create tools, share knowledge, develop its own expertise, and, eventually, become a real company operating within the metaverse itself.

That vision has evolved over the years, but the idea behind it never really changed.

## A Curious Moment in Time

It’s impossible to celebrate this anniversary without acknowledging the strange place Star Atlas finds itself in today.

On one hand, the project has endured some of its most difficult months. Much of the team has had to be let go as financial runway became increasingly constrained. Community sentiment has suffered, token prices have declined, and it’s understandable that many people have begun questioning where Star Atlas is heading.

Yet at exactly the same time, the game itself appears to be making some of its strongest progress.

Thanks in large part to AI, the remaining team has been able to move at a pace few of us expected. SAGE, and particularly the upcoming C4 expansion, is rapidly bringing many of the promises from the original 2021 white paper within reach. After years of imagining what a player-driven, 4X galactic economy could look like, it suddenly feels much closer than it did mere months ago.

## Five Years of Building

Aephia has experienced many of the same headwinds as the wider community. Members have come and gone, priorities have shifted, and we’ve had to make difficult decisions of our own.

One example was our **AEP Life Form** initiative. Alongside the NFT collection, we operated an investment club. As the broader crypto market continued to weaken, and expecting that trend to continue, we decided late last year to wind down the investment club. That decision wasn’t driven by Star Atlas, nor because we felt AEP Life Form had failed. It was simply the right decision given the market we found ourselves in. The collection itself remains part of Aephia, and we may reactivate it in some form in the future, if and when the timing is right.

What never really changed, however, was our desire to contribute.

Looking back over five years, I don’t think Aephia is defined by any single project. It’s defined by a pattern of continually trying to leave the ecosystem a little better than we found it.

## Five Years of Sharing

Knowledge sharing has always been part of Aephia’s DNA.

Over the years we’ve built community analytics, beginning with our early Flipside dashboards. When Flipside came to an end, we continued that work through [Atlas Dash](https://atlasdash.io). We created [Atlas Base](https://atlasbase.io) to make developer communication and governance information easier to navigate, built our Single Transferable Vote (STV) visualizer to bring more transparency to DAO elections, published gameplay guides, developed analytics, and continued developing tools for both our own members and the wider Star Atlas community.

This year also saw the launch of **[AstralPass.com](https://astralpass.com)**, our Discord and Solana verification platform. It was, like most things, originally built for Aephia, but is now available to any other guilds as well.

Week after week, year after year, we’ve simply kept showing up.

Those weeks eventually became **238 weekly newsletters**, **250 Sunday community meetings**, **81 Saturday strategy sessions**, and countless Unreal Engine 5 multiplayer events. None of those numbers were ever goals in themselves—they’re simply what happens when a community keeps showing up consistently.

Along the way, we’ve also been incredibly fortunate. Our members have collectively earned more **Joni Awards** than any other guild. Every official Star Atlas knowledge competition has been won by a current or former Aephia member. We’ve won COPA. We’ve hosted community meetups during Solana Breakpoint year after year.

Those aren’t achievements I mention to boast.

To me, they’re simply evidence of the kind of people this community attracts: people who enjoy learning, sharing what they’ve learned, and helping others do the same.

## Five Years… and Still Building

An anniversary isn’t only about looking back.

It’s also about looking ahead.

Much of our attention has already shifted toward **SAGE C4**. Behind the scenes, we’re developing a new generation of internal tools and services to help our members hit the ground running when C4 launches, while continuing to build resources and analytics that benefit the wider Star Atlas community.

And there’s one more thing I’m excited to finally share.

Many of you already know about **Cataclysm: Battle for Iris**, the 1v1 Gwent-inspired Star Atlas card game that Valkenen has been developing over the past few years. We originally hoped to reach this point sooner, but community-driven projects built in spare time have a habit of taking longer than expected.

The important part is that development never stopped.

Starting today, over the next two weeks, we’ll run an internal beta, after which we’ll open the game for a **public beta throughout the entire month of August**.

I can’t wait to finally see all of you playing it.

## Here’s to the Next Five Years

Five years ago, Aephia began with a simple idea.

Not just to participate in Star Atlas, but to help shape the ecosystem around it.

Looking back today, I’m incredibly proud—not only of the tools we’ve built, the newsletters we’ve written, the meetings we’ve hosted, or the projects we’ve launched, **but of the culture we’ve built together.**

A culture of curiosity.

A culture of contribution.

A culture of showing up.

Star Atlas will continue to evolve.

So will Aephia.

And as long as there are opportunities to build something useful, share something valuable, or bring this community together, I have every intention of continuing to do exactly that.

Thank you for being part of these first five years.

Here’s to the next five.
