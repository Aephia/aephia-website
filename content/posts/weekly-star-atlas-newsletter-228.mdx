## Holosim – Chapter 2 Rewards

John (Product Manager) finally published the **Holosim Season 1 Chapter 2** phase 1 prize list on Discord last week. The published pool lists **$8,815** worth of ships, which will be distributed to the **top 200** players. However, players who have a rank above 200, but have at least **reached 1,000,000 XP**, are set to receive a single Explorer Pack as well. The team set **Tuesday, May 5th**, as the distribution date, along with a mainnet wallet deadline.

That should have settled the matter, but not long after the announcement, community members pointed out a big discrepancy. Both the _Armstrong IMP Tap_ and the _Ogrika Tursic_ are medium-sized ships that both trade for less USDC on the marketplace than the large-sized _Opal BitBoat_.

Michael (CEO) acknowledged this, and the team is currently investigating. They are expected to publish an updated prize list that corrects this issue before distribution.

**Important**: If you haven’t already linked your primary wallet to your Holosim account, and you are eligible for prizes, you need to do this before Tuesday, May 5th (in other words, directly after reading this – if you read this on the day of publication)!

## UE5 Progress

Brad (Gameplay Engineer) joined José during [last week’s Atlas Brew ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Kir9SzaW6E)to show off some of the **UE5** progress teased on X over the past few weeks. The team has solved surface AI navigation on planets, meaning bots no longer have to be restricted to space stations or workarounds for interiors. That unlocks combat against enemies directly on the surface.

The first implementation of that work is a new **elimination mission** flow with three outposts, each carrying its own threat level and payout. On stream, Brad showed how those missions fit into the station menu, how stealth and alarm behavior work, and how this system opens the door to a more natural form of planetary combat than the team could support before.

Lastly, he showed off the new water/ocean renderer and how the waves affect a ship hovering above.

These updates are expected to roll out with the next UE5 update, though it is not yet clear when that will occur.

## DAO Updates

Last week, the voting window for **PIP-31** closed, with the official result being that the PIP was approved. Despite only 40% of voters being in favor, the large number of abstentions meant the Against side ended up with \~35% of the vote. Since there is no rule requiring at least 50% of the votes to approve a proposal, it officially passed.

However, in an unexpected turn of events, and a first for the Star Atlas DAO, the PIP’s creator withdrew the PIP hours from the voting window closing. This was discussed by the DAO Council and Michael (CEO), and they went along. As there is no way to pull a PIP from the system while being open for voting, the vote simply continued and passed.

So, while the PIP may have been technically approved, no ATLAS will be disbursed to PG, and the PIP will be effectively ignored by the DAO going forward.

### PIP-31: [Star Seekers 2 — Mobile Game DAO Funding Proposal](https://govern.staratlas.com/proposals/e71fb1a7-6d19-4565-8ff8-b4e79a3f8c16)

> This proposal requests 13,000,000 ATLAS from the Star Atlas DAO Ecosystem Fund to complete and launch Star Seekers 2, a cross-platform mobile space-combat game inspired by the Star Atlas universe, on both iOS App Store and Google Play Store.

Structure:

* **Requested funding:** 13,000,000 ATLAS
* **Structure**: 1-off PIP
* **Author**: PG Metaverse OG
* **Voting Opened**: Friday, April 17th
* **Voting Closes**: Friday, May 1st (end-of-day UTC)

Final results (final, \~134.5M PVP committed):

* **For**: 40.0%
* **Against**: 34.6%
* **Abstain**: 25.4%
