## Local Markets are Live!

This past week, the much-anticipated Local Markets were introduced to SAGE as part of the Dawn of the Freight update! This represents a big leap forward in SAGEâ€™s economic gameplay and adds a whole new layer of strategy to the game!

Local Markets bring a new approach to player-to-player trading within Star Atlas. Unlike the Galactic Marketplace, these markets operate within individual star systems, meaning all trades are confined to the system where they are initiated. This decentralization creates distinct regional economies where the availability of goods and prices can vary based on local supply and demand. As a result, players need to carefully consider the location of their trading activities and may find opportunities in moving goods between systems.

This change emphasizes the importance of logistics and strategic planning in the game. With trade now tied to specific locations, players will need to consider factors such as travel distances, fuel costs, and potential competition within a region. Local Markets mark a shift toward a more dynamic and immersive economy, making every system a potential hub of activity or scarcity, depending on player behavior.

[We wrote a Guide](https://aephia.com/star-atlas/sage-local-markets/) on Local Markets, providing more in-depth information and clear instructions on how to use this new feature.

## Faction Infrastructure Contracts (RFR) are Live!

Going live alongside Local Markets (albeit one day later), the team relaunched a new, upgraded version of their Request for Resources ATLAS emission mechanic, dubbed Faction Infrastructure Contracts! This new version comes with deeper in-game integration and is reliant on the new Local Marketplace functionality that launched alongside it.

These contracts allow players to craft specific items in-game and sell them back to the team for a fixed amount of ATLAS, providing a new avenue for earnings. Unlike the previous iteration, this new system doesnâ€™t impose a strict daily cap on the amount of FICs players can create, though theoretical limitations still exist due to one of the FICâ€™s ingredients, SDUs, being reliant on the scanning mechanics. This reintroduction aims to complement the existing Loyalty Point-based earnings, addressing issues where players within factions competed against each other for daily ATLAS allocations, which inadvertently discouraged recruitment.

[We also wrote a Guide](https://aephia.com/star-atlas/sage-infrastructure-contracts/) on Faction Infrastructure Contracts, which we recommend reading if this is all new to you.

_Note: At the time of publication, the initial supply of buy-orders ran out at some Starbases. There is, however, no hard limit to these. The team will replenish these at their earliest convenience!_

### Fees Glitch

As mentioned in [our guide](https://aephia.com/star-atlas/sage-infrastructure-contracts/), the team intended to levy a fee on FIC sales, similar to the sales fee on the Galactic Marketplace. However, due to a miscommunication, the first batch of FIC purchase orders was created without this fee applied.

Michael (CEO) and the team identified this, and they will attempt to remedy it with the next batch, which should go live later today (time of publication).
